How I Think

Strategy first.
Always.

I don't start with content. I start with the question: what do we actually want this person to do, feel, or believe? Everything else — format, copy, cadence — follows that answer.

Across brand strategy, social execution, and content planning, I've found that most marketing underperforms not because of bad creative, but because nobody agreed on the problem first.

My approach combines structured thinking with creative execution — I map the logic before I make the work. That's what I bring to every brief.

Case Study 01

Naturals NOW — 90-Day Marketing Plan

How do you launch Mumbai's only live-churning artisanal ice cream store and make the experience — not just the product — the point?

90
Day Plan
+3K
Target Followers
100+
UGC Posts Projected
5–8
PR Features Targeted

The Problem

Naturals NOW had a genuinely differentiated experience — live churning, botanical ambience, rotating menu. But experiential stores often fail to translate the IRL moment into lasting digital equity. The launch needed to make theatre the product.

The Insight

Mumbaikars don't just want good dessert. They want a shareable story. The live churning wasn't just a process — it was the content. Position it as Mumbai's most watchable food ritual, not just its freshest scoop.

The Strategy

  • 4 Content Pillars: The Churning Show · Flavor Drops · Garden Experience · Community UGC
  • Churning Hour Ritual: Daily 6–8 PM live events with customer photo moments and Story countdowns
  • #ChurnedNOW UGC campaign — best weekly video wins a free sundae
  • Monthly In-Store Activations: Flavor Lab Sundays → Garden Gigs → Artisan Collab Week

Collaborations

  • Coffee × Ice Cream fusion with premium coffee brands
  • Juhu Beach outdoor movie screening + bundled offers
  • Wellness Wednesdays with yoga studios — guilt-free framing
  • 3-tier influencer plan: food critics → micro-influencers → customer advocates

Case Study 02

EDT — Social Media Strategy

Building a content ecosystem for India's premium kitchen brand that doesn't sell appliances — it sells a way of living.

The Brief

EDT's LUMA air fryer is built with borosilicate glass bowls, a 56 dB motor, and engineering decisions that no one in the category talks about. The challenge: make technical superiority feel cultural and emotional, not spec-sheet cold.

Strategic Framework

Positioned EDT not as an appliance brand but as a cultural movement — Indian-made, world-class designed. Built five content buckets that each serve a different layer of the brand's equity.

5 Content Buckets

  • Material Truths — radical transparency on engineering decisions
  • The Invisible Upgrade — how design removes friction you didn't know you had
  • Ritual, Not Recipe — elevating cooking into an intentional practice
  • Designed for India, Built for the World — national pride meets global ambition
  • The Unboxing Feeling (But Make It Last) — UGC-driven long-term brand love

5 Creator Partnerships

  • Ruchira Hoon (MasterChef India) — Conscious Kitchen Series
  • Archit Agarwal — The Glass Bowl Series (aesthetic food photography)
  • Rishabh Telang (fitness/strength coach) — 48-Hour Meal Prep in LUMA
  • Mili Jhaveri (parenting) — The Real Week: Feeding Kids with LUMA
  • Nishant Choksi (New Yorker illustrator) — The New Indian Kitchen art series

3 Founder Content Themes

Built narrative arcs for the founder (Naiyya Sharma) across: The Decade Perspective (pattern recognition across BabyChakra → Good Glamm → EDT), The Unfiltered Build (real decision-making transparency), and The Advisor Conversations (intellectual leverage from OpenAI, Apple, and global advisors).
