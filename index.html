How I Think

Strategy first.
Always.

I don't start with content. I start with the question: what do we actually want this person to do, feel, or believe? Everything else — format, copy, cadence — follows that answer.

Across brand strategy, social execution, and content planning, I've found that most marketing underperforms not because of bad creative, but because nobody agreed on the problem first.

My approach combines structured thinking with creative execution — I map the logic before I make the work. That's what I bring to every brief.

🎯
Audience Before AestheticsUnderstand who you're talking to. Then decide how to say it.
🏗️

📊

Case Study 01 — Ice Cream Brand

Naturals NOW — 90-Day Marketing Plan

How do you launch Mumbai's only live-churning artisanal ice cream store and make the experience — not just the product — the entire point?

0
Day Plan
+3K
Target Followers
100+
UGC Posts Projected
5–8
PR Features Targeted

The Problem

Naturals NOW had a genuinely differentiated experience — live churning, botanical ambience, rotating menu. The launch needed to make theatre the product, not just an amenity.

The Insight

Mumbaikars don't just want good dessert. They want a shareable story. The live churning is the content. Position it as Mumbai's most watchable food ritual.

The Strategy

  • 4 Content Pillars: Churning Show · Flavor Drops · Garden Experience · UGC
  • Churning Hour Ritual — Daily 6–8 PM live events
  • #ChurnedNOW UGC campaign — best video wins free sundae
  • Monthly activations: Flavor Lab → Garden Gigs → Artisan Collabs

Collaborations

  • Coffee × Ice Cream fusion with premium brands
  • Juhu Beach outdoor movie screenings
  • Wellness Wednesdays with yoga studios
  • 3-tier influencer plan: critics → micro → advocates

Case Study 02 — Premium Appliance Brand

EDT — Social Media Strategy

Building a content ecosystem that doesn't sell appliances — it sells a way of living. Making technical superiority feel cultural and emotional, not spec-sheet cold.

The Brief

EDT's LUMA air fryer has borosilicate glass bowls, a 56 dB motor, and engineering decisions that no one in the category talks about. The challenge: make it feel cultural and emotional.

Strategic Frame

Position EDT not as an appliance brand — but as a cultural movement. Indian-made, world-class designed. Five content buckets each serving a different layer of brand equity.

5 Content Buckets

  • Material Truths — radical transparency on engineering
  • Invisible Upgrade — design that removes friction
  • Ritual, Not Recipe — cooking as intentional practice
  • Designed for India, Built for the World
  • Unboxing Feeling (Make It Last) — long-term UGC

5 Creator Partnerships

  • Ruchira Hoon (MasterChef India) — Conscious Kitchen
  • Archit Agarwal — Glass Bowl Series
  • Rishabh Telang (fitness coach) — 48-Hr Meal Prep
  • Mili Jhaveri (parenting) — The Real Week
  • Nishant Choksi (New Yorker) — Art collab
